Bed & Breakfasts, beach houses, and hostels available for every budget.

Getting Around Taniti

  • Arrive by air or cruise ship (most visitors arrive by air).
  • Public buses serve Taniti City from 5 a.m. to 11 p.m.
  • Private buses, taxis, and rental cars available for exploring the island.
  • Bikes and helmets available for rent; Taniti City is very walkable.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Power outlets are 120 volts (same as the US).
  • Alcohol is not served between midnight and 9:00 a.m.
  • Drinking age is 18, but not strictly enforced.
  • English is widely spoken in Taniti City; less so in rural areas.
  • US dollars, euros, and yen accepted; major credit cards welcome.
  • One hospital and several clinics serve the island.
  • Violent crime is rare, but watch for pickpocketing in tourist areas.
  • Many attractions and restaurants close on national holidays.
